Cost Optimization in Grading Operations — upGrad

Role: Project Manager · Focus: Cost efficiency & vendor management

Context / Challenge

Assessment grading was a significant recurring cost for upGrad programs. Grader A, the most reputed grader, was charging premium rates:

  • Short assignments: ₹100 per assessment
  • Long assignments (>2000 words): ₹500 per assessment
This flat-rate model led to high costs across cohorts and programs.

Approach / Execution

  • Onboarded two additional graders (Grader B & C) who had strong domain knowledge but were new to grading.
  • Negotiated a block-rate pricing model, scaling with assignment word count:
Assignment LengthRate (B & C)
≤1000 words₹100
≤2000 words₹200
≤3000 words₹300
≤4000 words₹400
≤5000 words₹500
  • Split grading work among A, B, and C to compare costs.
  • Showcased savings to stakeholders using sample data models.
  • Eventually negotiated Grader A down to the same block-rate model.

Results / Impact

Example calculation (120 assignments: 60 short, 60 long):

ScenarioTotal Cost
Before (all with Grader A)₹36,000
Mixed model (A, B, C)₹26,000
All negotiated to block rates₹21,000

✅ Initial cost saving: ~28%
✅ Final cost saving (after negotiation): ~41%

"This cost model, once scaled across cohorts and programs, led to significant savings in grading operations."

Transferable Insight

Strategic vendor onboarding, fair but competitive pricing models, and data-driven negotiations can drive substantial cost savings without compromising quality. These principles are directly applicable to SaaS vendor management and product operations.

Lessons Learned

  • Data-backed negotiation leads to sustainable vendor alignment.
  • Introducing new resources with transparent pricing models fosters healthy competition.
  • Scaling small wins (like this example) across the organization amplifies impact.
