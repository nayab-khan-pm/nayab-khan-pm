Cost Optimization in Grading Operations — Leading EdTech Provider

Role: Project Manager · Focus: Cost efficiency & vendor management

Context / Challenge

Assessment grading was a significant recurring cost for a leading EdTech provider's programs. One primary grader charged premium flat rates:

  • Short assignments: ₹100 per assessment
  • Long assignments (>2000 words): ₹500 per assessment
This flat-rate model drove high grading costs across cohorts and programs.

Approach / Execution

  • Onboarded two additional graders with strong domain knowledge but new to formal grading workflows.
  • Introduced a block-rate pricing model for the new graders, scaling rates with assignment word count:
Assignment LengthRate (New graders)
≤1000 words₹100
≤2000 words₹200
≤3000 words₹300
≤4000 words₹400
≤5000 words₹500
  • Modeled a mixed-grader allocation to compare cost outcomes when work was split among graders with different rate structures.
  • Presented sample financial scenarios to stakeholders to demonstrate potential cost savings at scale.
  • Negotiated existing grader rates over time to align to the block-rate model for sustainable savings.

Results / Impact

Example calculation (120 assignments: 60 short, 60 long):

ScenarioTotal Cost
Before (single premium grader)₹36,000
Mixed model (premium + 2 new graders)₹26,000
All aligned to block rates (negotiated)₹21,000

✅ Initial cost reduction using mixed allocation: ~28%
✅ Final cost reduction after negotiating premium grader to block rates: ~41%

"By introducing alternative graders with tiered pricing and negotiating legacy rates, the program achieved meaningful and sustainable cost efficiencies without sacrificing grading quality."

Transferable Insight

Strategic vendor onboarding, transparent pricing tiers, and data-driven negotiation can unlock significant recurring cost savings while preserving quality. This approach scales across cohorts and is readily applicable to product and operational vendor management in SaaS environments.

Lessons Learned

  • Run small financial pilots to validate new pricing models before broad rollout.
  • Combine objective cost modeling with quality checks to ensure vendor performance is maintained.
  • Negotiation anchored in data leads to sustainable alignment and amplified savings.
