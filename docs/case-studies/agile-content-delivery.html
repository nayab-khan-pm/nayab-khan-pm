Case Study: Agile Content Delivery & Release Management — Global Digital Learning Solutions Firm

Role: Project Manager · Duration: Sep 2022 – Aug 2023 · Scope: K–8 curriculum, ~390 modules (3 grades, 8 units per grade)

Context / Challenge

A global digital learning solutions firm was developing a large curriculum project (Grades 3–5). Each grade contained ~130 modules, grouped into 8 units, requiring alignment across Instructional Designers, Design, LMS, QA, US-based content teams, and curriculum directors. Stakeholders included delivery managers, directors, external vendors (audio), and end clients.

The challenge: ensure predictable delivery of hundreds of modules, coordinate across multiple functions, and handle dependencies like workbooks and audio — all within a one-year timeline.

Agile Approach / Execution

  • POC / MVP modules: Each unit began with a sample module as proof-of-concept, reviewed and approved by the client before scaling.
  • Sprint-based delivery: Modules were built in sprints per unit, following client-approved MVPs.
  • Release management: Each unit acted as a release — undergoing QA against a comprehensive checklist, with approvals across levels before going live.
  • Backlog management: Items that didn’t block release were logged into a Post-Activation Tracker for later maintenance sprints.
  • Time-sensitive sprint: A parallel sprint was run to deliver physical workbooks — ensuring publishing deadlines were met and penalties avoided.
  • Vendor integration: Managed audio vendor to replace AI voiceovers with professional recordings across all modules post-activation.

Results / Impact

  • Delivered ~390 modules across Grades 3–5 within a 12-month timeline.
  • Release-based activations allowed predictable delivery — stakeholders received usable increments per unit rather than waiting for full completion.
  • Post-Activation Tracker reduced rework during releases, balancing quality with on-time delivery.
  • On-time delivery of workbooks avoided penalty clauses and ensured client satisfaction.
  • Successful vendor management streamlined audio replacement across all modules post-launch.
"Structured Agile practices — MVPs, sprints, and incremental releases — gave stakeholders transparency and predictable outcomes in a complex multi-team project."
